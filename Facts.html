Fun Facts!

Did you know...

  1. The word poutine was commonly thought to come from the word pudding in English or the word pouding in French as it is.(Canadian Encyclopedia, 2019)
  2. In Québec the word poutine in slang means mess.(Canadian Encyclopedia, 2019)
  3. That by the 1970s a version of the dish was known as "Disco Fries", instead of regular cheese curds this dish uses shredded mozzarella instead.(Canadian Encyclopedia, 2019)
  4. During the year 2010 there was a poutine-eating contest in Toronto; the winner Pat "Deep Dish" Bertoletti of Chicago ate a total of 5.9 kg of poutine.(Macleans.ca, 2012)
  5. The largest poutine in the world was made in Saguenay,Québec and it weighed around 654 kg; that’s about as much as a large horse!(Macleans.ca, 2012)
  6. Poutine could get extremly expensive sometimes; depending on the type of poutine you get. Some of the most expensive poutines there is in the world is poutine au foie gras for $23 at Au Pied de Cochon in Montreal(Macleans.ca, 2012), a lobster poutine for $27 at Toronto’s Bymark Resturant, or a large poutine at Toronto’s Disgraceland with every single topping on the menu will get you a grand total of $85.(Cottagelife.com, 2014)
  7. Someone created a soda that tastes like poutine! A Seattle soda company decided to sell fries and gravy flavoured drinks; later on in 2013 after many experimentations with turkey and gravy flavouring they made the poutine flavoured soda.(dailyhive.com, 2022)
  8. Many canadians actually pronounce poutine wrong? Most canadians say Poo-Teen but in reality the correct way to call the dish is called Poot-In.(dailyhive.com, 2022)
  9. Poutine wasn’t actually always called Poutine, it was once called a mixte before it got renamed to Poutine.(Cottagelife.com, 2014)
  10. Newfoundland creating their own type of "poutine". They made it using chips, dressing, and gravy(known as stuffing) instead of the regular cheese curds in normal poutine.(Cottagelife.com, 2014)
  11. Poutine was actually listed in the top ten greatest inventions Canada has made by CBC in 2007.(Cottagelife.com, 2014)
